SEO for Financial Technologies (FinTech)

As we all know, for companies who rely on technology, SEO is one of the marketing channels to support your marketing activity. Winning the market share and becoming the market leader is every company’s ultimate goal. But first, you need to introduce your brand to the audience. Since FinTech niches are facing tough competition this last decade, one of the effective ways to reach and get recognition is by implementing SEO.

SEO can be considered a long-term investment, and able to save your marketing budget compared to PPC/SEM. Imagine, if you want to have 1000 daily visitors to your website, and you target 1 keyword that has Cost Per Click (CPC) for 15k, that means you need to spend 15 million per day. But in SEO, with the same total spend, you are able to target 2k visitors per day.

So what’s the correlation between using SEO and FinTech business strategies? People are now becoming vigilant before they choose a brand, moreover if the brand has a critical value to their life. It is called Your Money Your Life (YMYL) Pages. Some business niche needs to ensure that their website provides comprehensive, reliable (verified and recorded in The Financial Services Authority), trustworthy content and information.

FinTech websites are one of the YMYL pages. Before people consider the brand that fits their needs, they used to check its brand reputation and maybe some reviews (this is where websites have an essential role). So, they went to search engines and type questions related to the brand's information or general information to see if your website has the answer. So it’s really important to make your website visible every time they are curious about financial matters. Website visibility refers to how well your website can appear on the Search Engine Result Page (SERP), precisely on the 1st page.

Why appear on the 1st page can change their perspective towards your brand? Well, it is quite relevant to people's behavior online. They tend to seek the easiest way and make a quick decision when it comes to scanning the perfect match for a brand that can solve their problems or questions. They have no interest in observing so many websites on search engines, oftentimes, they only scroll their screen to the 2nd page on SERP. That’s why your website must be capable of catching general keywords and queries.

Let’s talk about the most popular search engine all over the world, Google. Google has declared that they have some sort of assessment and principle to serve users with great and relevant content. It is called Google E-A-T, which stands for Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google E-A-T appeared on Google Search Quality Rating Guidelines. With this principle, we understand that every published content must be well written by responsible Writers due to generating more organic traffic. By that means, the Writers involved in the content creation process must be verified in the specific field, i.e Certified Financial Advisor. But what if the experts could not accommodate and are incapable of writing SEO-Friendly Content?

cmlabs will take part as the SEO Specialist who is responsible for doing the keyword research for each topic and as the SEO Content Writer who is in charge of producing content on your website. Then your Certified Financial Advisor will be the content reviewer, which ensures that the information given to users is adequate, comprehensive, valid, and credible. This scheme can be implemented vice versa, where your Certified Financial Advisor will produce the content on your website, and cmlabs’ SEO Content Writer will review the content’s SEO aspects and give a free edit before publishing.

The workflow for this multi-team collaboration will be written in the Quotation Letter and Service Level Agreement (SLA). We have experience working with a FinTech company, see their website performance here!
